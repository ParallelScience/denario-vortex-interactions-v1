The failure to observe a strong correlation between Lagrangian deformation and vortex lifetime (Pearson r = -0.032) and the significant deviation of the energy spectrum (k^-0.625) from the expected Kolmogorov scaling (k^-1.667) are directly attributable to the dataset's resolution constraints.

1. **Resolution/Grid Size**: The 128³ grid is insufficient to resolve the inertial range of the turbulence, as evidenced by the energy spectrum slope. Because the vortex cores are small structures, a 128³ grid provides limited spatial sampling of the velocity gradient tensor within the cores. This leads to high numerical noise in the calculation of the strain-to-rotation ratio ($\alpha$), masking the physical relationship between local strain and vortex stability.
2. **Measurement Noise/Aggregation**: The use of `np.gradient` on a coarse 128³ grid to derive the velocity gradient tensor $A_{ij}$ introduces discretization errors. Since the Lagrangian deformation analysis relies on these derivatives to compute $\alpha$, the resulting metric is likely dominated by numerical artifacts rather than the underlying physical strain, explaining the lack of correlation with vortex lifetime.
3. **Time Cadence**: The stride of 5 (Δt = 0.05) between snapshots is relatively large compared to the mean vortex lifetime (0.613). This temporal undersampling limits the ability to accurately integrate the deformation rate along trajectories, further contributing to the weak correlation observed in the Lagrangian analysis.
