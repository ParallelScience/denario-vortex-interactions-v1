- Decision: ITERATE
- Justification: The current project has reached a "conceptual wall" where the point-particle/Langevin framework is failing to capture the underlying physics of 3D turbulence. However, the data and the existing pipeline are high-quality and contain the necessary information to pivot the *theoretical focus* without abandoning the project entirely. 

The "Actionable Recommendations" provided are not just minor tweaks; they represent a fundamental shift from a "Gas-Kinetic" model (which is physically inappropriate for 3D turbulence) to a "Lagrangian Structural" model. This shift is highly desirable because:
1. **Scientific Impact**: Moving from a failed potential-fitting exercise to a study of vortex stretching ($\vec{\omega} \cdot \nabla \vec{u}$) and core stability under strain aligns the paper with modern turbulence research (e.g., the study of "worms" or intense coherent structures).
2. **Publishability Delta**: Reviewers in fluid dynamics will likely reject a paper that forces a Langevin/potential model onto 3D turbulence data. Conversely, a paper that rigorously characterizes the stability of vortex cores using Lagrangian budget analysis and conditional averaging of the velocity gradient tensor is a strong candidate for journals like *Physical Review Fluids* or *Journal of Fluid Mechanics*.
3. **ROI**: The infrastructure (tracking, Q-criterion, trajectory extraction) is already built. The "pivot" is in the analysis layer—replacing the regression of $V(r)$ with the calculation of strain-vorticity correlations and conditional averages. This is a high-reward, moderate-effort transition that salvages the existing data while significantly increasing the scientific rigor and "So What?" factor of the final manuscript.
