- Decision: ITERATE
- Justification: The current project has reached a critical "Publishability Delta." The initial results were descriptive and plagued by tracking artifacts, but the current iteration has successfully uncovered genuine physical phenomena: helicity-dependent Biot-Savart attraction and a counterintuitive relationship between strain and vortex longevity. These findings are scientifically significant and move the paper from a simple data-processing exercise to a contribution in turbulence theory. 

The proposed changes are not "unnecessary complexity"; they are essential for technical integrity. Specifically, replacing the greedy tracker with a predictive (Kalman/Hungarian) approach is mandatory to resolve the Lévy index ambiguity, which is currently the weakest point of the manuscript. Furthermore, the shift from Pearson correlations to a Langevin-based drift model for the interaction potential is a standard, robust requirement for high-tier physics journals. The ROI of this effort is high because the data is already processed and the "story" is emerging; the remaining work is to refine the methodology to ensure the conclusions are bulletproof against reviewer scrutiny. The project is well-positioned to provide a novel, high-fidelity sub-grid scale parameterization, which is a high-impact outcome.
