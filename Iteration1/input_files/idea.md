**Title**: Lagrangian Deformation Dynamics of Vortex Cores in Driven Isotropic Turbulence

**Description**: This study characterizes the structural evolution of coherent vortices by mapping the Lagrangian history of the Okubo-Weiss parameter ($Q$) and the velocity gradient tensor eigenvalues along centroid trajectories. By tracking vortex centers across the 200-snapshot sequence, we compute the time-integrated deformation rate experienced by each vortex, using the local strain-to-rotation ratio ($|S|/|\Omega|$) to quantify the transition from rotation-dominated cores to strain-dominated filaments. We will correlate the evolution of the vortex-specific circulation $\Gamma$ with the cumulative stretching experienced along its trajectory, identifying the critical strain threshold at which topological integrity is lost. This approach replaces binary lifetime metrics with a continuous deformation metric, effectively capturing the physical processes of vortex stretching and shredding within the constraints of the 128³ grid. The result is a robust empirical model relating background solenoidal driving to vortex-core stability, providing a high-fidelity basis for sub-grid scale parameterization in turbulence modeling.
